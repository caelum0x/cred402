# p7 — ML risk-engine v2

> **Learn creditworthiness from realized outcomes, not just rules.** Add a trained
> probability-of-default model on top of the hand-tuned v1 policy, and blend the two
> so credit decisions improve as the protocol accumulates repayment/default history.

## Why
The v1 risk policy (`risk_policy.ts`) is a transparent rules engine — great for
launch and auditability, but it can't *learn*. As Cred402 accrues realized credit
outcomes (who repaid, who defaulted), the cheapest accuracy gain is a model fit to
that history. Keeping the rules score alongside the learned score preserves
explainability and gives a safe fallback.

## Scope
- **Feature model.** A stable, normalized feature vector per agent: reputation,
  accuracy, dispute rate, experience (job count), recent revenue, stake, and
  service-category risk.
- **Learned PD model.** A logistic-regression probability-of-default classifier,
  fit by batch gradient descent on labeled outcomes. Deterministic and dependency-
  free (no external ML runtime), so it is reproducible and the weights are
  inspectable/auditable.
- **Blend, don't replace.** `blended_score = α·ml_score + (1−α)·rules_score`, so
  v2 augments v1 rather than overriding it. A hand-calibrated prior makes the model
  useful before any training data exists.

## Deliverables (shipped)
- `lib/services/risk_engine_v2.ts` — `RiskEngineV2`:
  - `extractFeatures(agent)` → normalized `[0,1]` `FeatureVector`.
  - `predictPd(features)` → probability of default.
  - `train(samples, opts)` → fits weights by gradient descent on log-loss
    (deterministic; L2-regularized).
  - `score(agentId)` → `{ pd, ml_score, rules_score, blended_score, risk_band }`.
  - `PRIOR_WEIGHTS` — domain-calibrated prior; `weights()` exposes the model.
- `test/risk_engine_v2.test.ts` — prior ranking, training reduces log-loss + learns
  signals, blended score, normalization, determinism.

## Success metric
The blended score out-predicts the rules-only score on held-out realized outcomes,
while every decision remains explainable (rules score + inspectable model weights).
