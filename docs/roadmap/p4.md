# p4 — Mainnet-beta credit market

> **Turn the simulation into real working capital — carefully.** Onboard liquidity
> providers, set conservative risk caps, stand up an insurance reserve, and launch a
> controlled mainnet beta with approved operators and service categories.

## Why
Everything before this proves the loop with test value. Real credit is the point —
but real lending is regulated-finance territory. Launch must be deliberate: small
limits, real collateral/stake coverage, honest risk dashboards, and governance
guardrails before any uncapped exposure.

## Scope
- **Liquidity.** LP deposit/withdraw on mainnet, pool shares, real interest accrual,
  origination/late fees, honest realized-APY reporting (no fake APY).
- **Risk controls.** Per-agent and per-category exposure caps, minimum reputation +
  seasoning gates, stake coverage, automatic freezes, an **insurance reserve**, and
  the 50/25/25 slash routing already modeled.
- **Risk engine in the loop.** The Python PD model + Tarjan-SCC fraud graph become
  advisory inputs to `RiskPolicyManager`; high-fraud agents are refused.
- **Governance & safety.** Launch multisig + timelock, emergency pause, public
  parameter history, approved-operator allowlist, KYB above thresholds
  (RealFi/`OperatorVerificationRegistry`), legal/operating model review.

## Deliverables
- Mainnet contract deployment + verified addresses + runbooks (pause/unpause,
  default handling, key rotation).
- LP onboarding flow + risk dashboard (utilization, loss rate, reserve, defaults).
- Risk-engine → policy wiring; fraud gating live; insurance reserve funded.

## Success metric
First real (or guarded mainnet-beta) credit lines **drawn and repaid**, with
positive LP yield **after** defaults and zero uncovered losses.

## Out of scope
Cross-chain lending (p5), full decentralization/token (p6).
